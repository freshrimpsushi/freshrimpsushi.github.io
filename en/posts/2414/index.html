The expected difficulty level is suitable for 2nd to 3rd-year undergraduates.

Graph Group

Let’s represent the set of $n$ labeled simple graphs as $\mathbb{G}_{n}$. For instance, when $n = 5$, an element $G \in \mathbb{G}_{n}$ can look like this:

Here, the binary operation $G , F \in \mathbb{G}_{n}$ on $\oplus$ is defined in such a way that if an edge $(i,j)$ exists in either $G$ or $F$, it also exists in $G \oplus F$, and if $(i,j)$ is present in both, it does not exist in $G \oplus F$.

Moreover, any graph $G$ operated with the null graph $E \in \mathbb{G}_{n}$ by $\oplus$ results in $G$ itself.

Also, operating a graph with itself yields the null graph, i.e., $G \oplus G = E$. From what we have seen, $\left( \mathbb{G}_{n} , \oplus \right)$ intuitively appears to form a group. The first task is to prove that it indeed forms a group, and further, an abelian group.

Tasks

Solution Task

Demonstrate that $\left( \mathbb{G}_{n} , \oplus \right)$ is an abelian group. In other words, prove that $\oplus$

  1. satisfies the associativity,
  2. commutativity,
  3. has an identity element, and
  4. has an inverse

Research Task

Propose an operation $\otimes$ that might allow $\left( \mathbb{G}_{n} , \oplus , \otimes \right)$ to become a ring and prove that it indeed forms a ring. Explore its structure. For instance, find theorems that hold for specific $n$s or for $\mathbb{G}_{n}$s that are isomorphic to another ring and prove each of them.

  • (22.02.26) Conditions relaxed from a field to a ring

Details

Evaluation Criteria

Solution tasks will be evaluated based on succinctness and clarity, while research tasks will be assessed on the depth of properties uncovered. Among submissions that devise the same field, the one discovering more theorems will be declared the winner.

Submission Period

Submissions are accepted from January 1st, 2022 until March 31st, 2022. Entries submitted after this deadline, based on Korean Standard Time, will not be accepted.

Submission

The format for both solution and research tasks does not matter. You may solve them manually on paper and submit scans, or compose your answers with $\TeX$ and send them as a PDF. Submissions in English are also accepted.

  • Solution Task submissions should be sent to freshrimpsushi@gmail.com. This is to prevent later participants from copying the answers of those who submit earlier.
  • Research Task submissions are to be openly published on the community (https://freshrimpsushi.com/community/) personal research board. This is to avoid overlaps in ideas among participants and to credit the contributions of pioneers if similarities arise.
